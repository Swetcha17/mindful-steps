Walkability Index

The Walkability Index is a comprehensive score that helps us measure how friendly, accessible, and safe a walking route is across the Lehigh campus. It’s based on 26 indicators grouped into six core domains: Accessibility, Aesthetics, Land Use, Safety, Transportation, and Intersections. These indicators include everything from surface type, slope, and path maintenance to trees, greenery, lighting, and crosswalk quality.

Each segment is audited and scored on a 1–5 scale using validated tools like SPACES, PEAT, and SWEAT. For example, a smooth, continuous concrete path with shade, good lighting, benches, and crosswalks would score highly. In contrast, a steep, broken sidewalk with poor lighting or no pedestrian buffers would score lower. The goal is to offer mindful, comfortable walking experiences while addressing accessibility and safety for all users.
